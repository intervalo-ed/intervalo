## Ubiquitous language

- Course: like a college course or high school subject. Each course is divided into belts.
- Belts: every course has 5 belts like in jiu jitsu (white, blue, purple, brown, black). They are used to organize the content of the course, from basics and fundamentals to advanced, complex and more practical topics. Each belt is divided into topics.
- Topics: specific type of content within a belt (e.g.: "Linear functions", "Quadratic functions", etc.). A belt can have few or many topics, it's up to the course creator.
- Exercises: a question with options and a correct answer. Each exercise is associated with a topic.

- Learning phase: the phase where the student is learning the content of a topic. Usually takes between 2 and 7 days.
- Reviewing phase: the phase where the student already know the content of a topic, but needs to review it after a certain amount of time to avoid forgetting it. The repetition interval increases over time.

- Mastering a topic: when a student finishes the learning phase of a topic and starts the reviewing phase.
